| Page 3 of 6 CGIAR 1. Introduction Interest in agroecology is booming. Over the past decade, multilateral organizations, national governments and international research institutes have turned to agroecology as a promising way to build more sustainable and inclusive food systems. Social movements have promoted and adopted agroecology for several decades due, in part, to governments largely being unwilling or uninterested in agroecological research and supportive public policies. The notable exception is in Cuba where the government turned to agroecology as the foundation for a new food system in the wake of the Soviet Union’s collapse. Their transition to agroecology was out of necessity because they were no longer able to rely on industrial agricultural inputs from the Soviets. Cuba’s wholesale endorsement of agroecology, and their successes in transitioning, has been studied extensively, including the role of the state, farmer-to-farmer knowledge sharing and urban agriculture (c.f., Machín Sosa et al., 2013). However, an often-overlooked aspect of agroecology is the role of National Agricultural Research Systems (NARS) to enable technological development and to maintain public goods for widespread agroecological transitions at scale. While this research project doesn’t look to Cuba as a model, it does aim to better understand the role of NARS in leveraging long-term changes for more sustainable and socially just food systems.The overarching question that drives the research is: what would an agroecological NARS look like and how can such a system be taken up across national contexts? This research project will draw on research in Peru, Tunisia, India and Vietnam to initiate long-term applied research collaborations for institutional and policy change towards agroecologically-based NARS. It consists of a two-pronged approach to analyze and guide the role of NARS for agroecology worldwide: 1. Analysis on NARS in select countries to determine the key factors driving research agendas 2. Collaborative workshops to generate guidelines for NARS institutional and policy change based around agroecological principles and alternatives to conventional agriculture 3. A further expansion of the research into new geographies and the incorporation of landscape- level multifunctionality within NARS research agendas where appropriate (from 2027) 2. Methods and timeline The two methods for part 1 (NARS Analysis) include Key Informant Interviews (KIIs) and document analysis. Below is a brief description of each for initial guidelines. KIIs: are used to gather in-depth insights from individuals with specialized institutional knowledge (Akhter, 2022). The KIIs for this project will be based on a semi-structured approach with an interview guide co-developed with country teams. Additionally, participants will be selected based on criteria co-developed with country teams. The aim is for each country to have at least 10 key informant interviews with people from NARS and those involved in agenda setting for NARS (e.g., Ministry of Agriculture). Document analysis: official documents describe the primary policies and regulations for NARS. These documents may be from the NARS themselves or the Ministry of Agriculture. The initial selection of documents will focus on key reports and policies such as: annual reports, financial documents, agenda setting and secondary literature (e.g., journal articles analyzing NARS for a given country).

The initial workshop will provide the opportunity to present the results from part 1 and gather feedback. The second workshop will focus on collectively generating guidelines for institutional change. Participants will be selected based on their identity (including race, gender and discipline) and position within the NARS to have a range of representation. Some potential methods for the workshop include, but are not limited to: 1) World Café; 2) Participatory Mapping; 3) Role-Playing; 4) Brainstorming Sessions; 5) Action Planning Part 2: Illustrative Methods for Collaborative NARS Reforms NARS-AE Research Activity Timeline Research activity Q2 2025 Q3 2025 Q4 2025 Q1 2026 Q2 2026 Q3 2026 Q4 2026 Part 1 Part 2
